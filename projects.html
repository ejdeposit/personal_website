Star Battle Solver

I first encountered Star Battle puzzles in the New York Times where they were printed under the name Two Not Touch. The puzzle board is divided into regions. In each region, row, and column there can be no more than two stars, and stars can not be next to adjacent stars. I wondered what sort of algorithms could solve them. Luckily the next term I started learning about these algorithms in my Artificial Intelligence course.

I implemented the AC-3 constraint satisfaction algorithm to solve these puzzles. The alogithm a back-tracking search for a solution. At each step, it looks eliminates any possible next moves that are no longer feasible and checks to make sure that each board is not impossible to solve given the current configuration. If it is, it backtracks and try another feasible move.
